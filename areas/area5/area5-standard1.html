Criterion 1. A system with defined plans, structures, and policies is established for proper recruitment, admission, and placement of learners.

Rating: 4.5

The Ateneo de Davao University Senior High School has a system for recruitment, admission, and placement of learners. The Admission and Aid Office manages both the recruitment and admission of students, guided by the structured framework and policies in the SHS - Manual of Operations that ensure a careful process in recruiting students, admitting qualified individuals, and promoting students from Grade 11 to Grade 12.

The office also follows a dynamic Marketing Plan that guides the strategic implementation of campaigns. Recruitment initiatives include school visits, caravans, open house events, social media campaigns, and online pre-registration to actively engage prospective students.

The admission system follows the requirements, policies, and procedures in compliance with the Department of Education and the university standards and qualifications.

The Wellness and Testing Center handles the placement of learners. For a systematic placement, students undergo Placement Tests (SHS-Career Readiness Assessment) and Diagnostic Exams in Math and English to determine readiness, address learning gaps, and align learners with suitable academic and career pathways.

Supporting Evidences:

  • SHS Manual of Operations
  • Marketing Plan
  • SHS Readiness Assessment
  • Recruitment Initiatives

Criterion 2. Criteria for student selection and placement are defined to predict learners’ potential in the educational program and promote proper matching of student aptitudes and capabilities to their levels/programs.

Rating: 4.5

The student’s selection and placement criteria are defined in the Admission Policies and Placement Policies of the Senior High School’s Manual of Operations.

During enrollment, learners and their parents are oriented on the curriculum, academic strands, pathways, and electives to support informed decision-making. The Wellness and Testing Center conducts interviews, diagnostic tests, and appraisal services to assess mental ability, aptitude, interests, and learning styles, while maintaining individual inventory records to guide placement and career pathways. Policies on strand placement and strand shifting after Grade 11 further promote proper matching, while enrichment programs and career guidance services provide additional support.

These criteria were used in promoting learners from one grade level to another to ensure their successful completion of the Senior High School program.

Criterion 3. Defined procedures are implemented to ensure effective implementation of recruitment, admission, and placement of learners.

Rating: 4.5

The Ateneo de Davao University Senior High School (AdDU-SHS) implemented a structured approach to recruitment, admission, and placement as outlined in its Manual of Operations. Furthermore, the Marketing Plan was carried out through school visits, caravans, an online website, publicity materials, and open house events, among others, in effectively reaching and engaging prospective students.

For admission, the enrollment procedures were clearly detailed and communicated to applicants, ensuring they were well-prepared and that clear expectations were set for a smooth process.

Lastly, for placement, tests were administered and results were interpreted to learners and parents in an orderly manner, ensuring a systematic evaluation of students’ abilities and aptitudes to guide them toward the most suitable academic pathways.

Supporting Evidences:

  • Manual of Operations
  • Marketing Plan
  • Admission/Enrollment Procedures
  • Learner’s Manual - Appendix H Admission Process
  • Placement Tests (Schedule, announcement, process)

Criterion 4. Programs and plans are in place to monitor the system’s effectiveness for recruitment, admission, and placement of learners.

Rating: 4.0

The Ateneo de Davao University Senior High School (AdDU-SHS) ensures the effectiveness of its recruitment, admission, and placement system through established programs and monitoring mechanisms.

For recruitment, the Admissions and Aid Office implements an annual Recruitment Plan that includes school visits, open house orientations, and digital campaigns. These initiatives are monitored through application tracking data, number of enrollees from each feeder school, and feedback from parents and students during enrollment.

For admissions, the process is governed by the Admission Policies and Procedures in the SHS Manual of Operations. Admission statistics, acceptance-to enrollment conversion rates, and entrance profile reports are generated and analyzed to evaluate admission effectiveness.

For placement, the Wellness and Testing Center administers placement tests, interviews, and appraisal services, while maintaining individual inventory records. Strand placement and shifting policies are monitored through reports on strand-shifting cases, academic performance per strand, and retention rates. Results are presented to the administrators and used to refine placement policies and guidance programs.

Supporting Evidences:

  • Marketing Event Evaluation Forms
  • Open House Evaluation Forms
  • Enrollment Evaluation Forms
  • Placement Test Results Analysis

Criterion 5. Student recruitment, admission, and placement are improved to ensure that they remain relevant and effective.

Rating: 4.5

The Ateneo de Davao University Senior High School (AdDU-SHS) continuously refines its recruitment, admission, and placement processes to ensure relevance and effectiveness. Recruitment strategies are updated through a dynamic Marketing Plan and evaluation of previous campaigns. Admission procedures, guided by the Manual of Operations, are reviewed regularly to respond to learner needs and educational trends. Placement policies are enhanced through the results of diagnostic tests, career guidance sessions, strand-shifting evaluations, and feedback from students and parents. These refinements ensure that the system remains responsive, student-centered, and aligned with institutional goals.

Supporting Evidences:

  • University Fair and Publicity Materials
  • Centralized Marketing Team Memoramdum
  • One Basic Education Publicity Materials
  • Recent Promotional Campaigns/Publicity Materials
  • Revised Placement Tests
