Area 2: Quality Assurance

Sub-area 2.1 Internal and External Quality Assurance System

Standard 6.
The school has an established quality assurance system that includes internal provisions for ensuring and enhancing the quality of its educational programs and processes. Periodically, the school also subjects itself to an external assessment that serves to validate its internal quality assurance system against acceptable standards of quality for basic education.

Criterion 1. The school has an established internal quality assurance system that includes clearly defined policies, procedures, and activities designed to ensure and continuously enhance the quality of its academic and non-academic programs and processes.

Rating: 4.0

The Ateneo de Davao University has an established internal quality assurance system that encompasses its Academic, Administrative, and Formation Programs Manuals. These program manuals were first rooted in the One Ateneo, One Plan (1A1P) Strategic Plan for 2018-2023 and were further strengthened by the formal establishment of the Office of Strategic Management and Quality Assurance (OSMQA), with defined mandates and functions. The implementation of these systems continuously enhances the University’s operations, leading to measurable improvements in the quality of its academic and non-academic programs, services, and processes.

For Academic Year 2025–2026, the IQA system is aligned with the Fortiores 2030, which was developed through a strategic, synodal, and spiritual process that promotes shared ownership, collaborative decision-making, and collective discernment across all sectors of the University. Under the leadership of OSMQA, the IQA system ensures sustained quality enhancement through the consistent application of clearly articulated policies, systematic and strategic procedures, and evidence-based activities.

The University’s Senior High School (SHS) Unit has standardized operational processes for both its academic and non-academic programs. These processes are documented in the AdDU SHS Learners’ Manual, and the manual of the different SHS Offices. which clearly articulates policies and procedures that are systematically structured, compliant with national standards, and aligned with the university’s educational values and strategic commitments. It serves as a definitive guide for the implementation, monitoring, and evaluation of SHS programs, ensuring consistency, transparency, and accountability in its operations.

Criterion 2. The different sectors of the school community and other stakeholders are involved in the formulation, implementation, and evaluation of internal quality assurance strategies and processes.

Rating: 4.0

The University ensures the active involvement of its various sectors in the formulation of its internal quality assurance strategies and processes. Anchored on the university’s One Ateneo, One Plan (1A1P) Strategic Plan for 2018-2023, the ratification, implementation, and evaluation of the plan were undertaken through broad-based participation of stakeholders across academic and administrative units. The AFIRE (Administration, Formation, Instruction, Research, and Engagement) framework provided the structural foundation for these processes, ensuring institutional alignment.

Under the new leadership of the university in 2024, the extent of stakeholder participation has been intensified through the Synodal, Spiritual, and Strategic processes, which provided opportunities for all members of the community to express their views and perspectives.

Criterion 3. A structure for internal quality assurance is in place with specific roles, responsibilities, and accountabilities for all concerned.

Rating: 4.0

The University’s organizational structure aligns with its stated vision and mission as articulated in One Ateneo, One Plan (1A 1P), following the pillars of AFIRE, whose chairs report internal quality assurance to the Vice President for Planning and Quality Assurance.

With the new leadership, the internal quality assurance is now the oversight of the Office of the Strategic Management Quality Assurance, wherein an Internal Quality Assurance (IQA) Officer oversees the internal quality operations of the academics. Specifically, in the basic ed unit, the IQA is put under the supervision of the Vice President for Basic Education, who closely coordinates with the IQA Officer of the OSMQA.

The Office of the Strategic Management Quality Assurance was established in 2024 to provide structure and facilitation for strategic development and implementation. It coordinates and centralizes initiatives aimed at enhancing the university’s commitment to quality and mission (Fortiores 2030 ADDU Strategic Plan 2025-2030).

Criterion 4. Measurable targets and indicators are used to assess the school’s performance against internal quality assurance goals.

Rating: 4.0

The Ateneo de Davao University Senior High School’s (ADDU SHS) performance is assessed based on the extent of its achievement of the interventions of the One Ateneo, One Plan (1A1P). These interventions are annually reported to the VPQA through the chairs of the AFIRE.

In transition to the Fortiores 2030 ADDU Strategic Plan 2025-2030, the SHS unit has developed its operational plan, which aligns with the University’s strategic priorities. This operational plan incorporates clearly defined strategic initiatives, encompassing outcomes, outputs, and measurable indicators and targets, which serve as the basis for systematically assessing institutional and unit-level performance.

Criterion 5. mplementing internal quality assurance strategies and processes are reviewed periodically to ensure the continuous improvement of programs and operations.

Rating: 3.7

The Senior High School’s operational plan undergoes a regular review to ensure alignment with the University’s institutional goals and established quality standards. Guided by the Fortiores 2030 and the school’s internal calendar, the Senior High School Unit evaluates its internal quality assurance strategies and processes to enhance the effectiveness of its programs and operations. These evaluations, conducted through institutionalized annual planning and assessment sessions, ensure the effectiveness, relevance, and enhancement of the unit’s programs and operations.

Standardized tools such as internal evaluation tools, evaluation reports, monitoring sheets, and classroom observations are utilized during the reviews. These tools help the school track the implementation of its quality assurance strategies against its alignment with the established University’s institutional goals and established quality standards.

In alignment with the University’s strategic framework, Fortiores 2030, periodic reviews are embedded within the institutional cycle of planning, implementation, monitoring, and evaluation to ensure consistency and quality across units. These periodic reviews of programs across all units will be implemented through the MEAL Plan (Monitoring, Evaluation, Accountability, and Learning), to strengthen the culture of continuous improvement.

Criterion 6. The school subjects itself to periodic external quality assessment to validate its internal quality assurance system against acceptable quality standards for basic education.

Rating: 4.0

The Senior High School (SHS), being the youngest among the three schools of the University’s Basic Education cluster, has successfully transitioned from the challenges of the pandemic. It is now ready to undergo external quality assurance processes.

The school ensures that its internal mechanisms are objectively measured against national standards for basic education, which include academic and non-academic personnel profiles and curriculum implementation. While its operations are internally quality-assured in accordance with the minimum standards prescribed by the Department of Education, the SHS is preparing to further subject its programs and services to external review through the certification process of the Private Education Assistance Committee (PEAC) and the accreditation process of the Philippine Accrediting Association of Schools, Colleges, and Universities (PAASCU).

Criterion 7. The external assessment is evidence-based and conducted by credible independent external agencies.

Rating: 3.5

In alignment with the external quality assurance mechanisms already established in the Grade School and Junior High School through the Philippine Accrediting Association of Schools, Colleges, and Universities (PAASCU) and the Private Education Assistance Committee (PEAC), the Senior High School entrusts its external assessments to these independent and credible agencies.

While the SHS is yet to formally undergo an accreditation visit with PAASCU, preparations for the forthcoming visit are already in progress. The establishment of the PAASCU Executive Committee ensures the systematic coordination of tasks, monitoring of requirements, and implementation of preparatory activities necessary for the accreditation process.
