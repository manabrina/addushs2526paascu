Criterion 1. The school has a community engagement and service program that is aligned with its vision, mission, goals, objectives, and core values.

Rating: 4

The school has a well-defined Community Engagement and Service Program implemented through the Arrupe Office for Social Formation. This program is anchored on the Pakighinabi – Pamalandong – Adbokasiya Framework, which moves students from dialogue and encounter (*Pakighinabi*), to reflection and discernment (*Pamalandong*), and finally to concrete action (*Adbokasiya*). Guided by this framework, the program ensures that students are formed to listen deeply, reflect critically, and act responsibly in response to the needs of the community.

Aligned with the school’s vision, mission, and core values, the Community Engagement and Service Program is committed to the formation of discerning leaders, the promotion of active citizenship, and the advancement of peace and development, with particular focus on Mindanao.

Criterion 2. Community engagement and service activities are implemented to promote the development of both the members of the school community and the partner community.

Rating: 4

The Arrupe Office for Social Formation implements community engagement and service activities for various members of the school and partner communities. It appropriates its activities for three sectors: academic, formation, and teachers’ community engagement.

At the academic level, all Grade 12 students are required to participate in community engagements integrated with the subjects Leadership Development Program and Catholic Social Teachings. At the formation level, student clubs are encouraged to initiate outreach projects that foster social awareness, responsibility, and solidarity. Meanwhile, teachers participate in community service activities that align their professional expertise and personal skills with the identified needs and priorities of partner communities.

Through this process, students and teachers develop leadership, empathy, and social responsibility, while partner communities benefit from concrete assistance, capacity-building, and sustained partnerships. In this way, community engagement promotes mutual growth enriching the learning and formation of the school community while fostering empowerment and development in the communities served.

Criterion 3. Community engagement and service activities utilize and develop the competencies of the different sectors of the school community.

Rating: 3.8

The school promotes multi-sectoral involvement in its community engagement programs. The Arrupe Office for Social Formation ensures that students, faculty, administrators, and community stakeholders contribute meaningfully based on their roles and expertise.

The activities are intentionally designed as a dynamic platform for both the utilization and development of competencies across all participating sectors. For students, the program utilizes their existing communication skills in roles like facilitating and documenting, while actively developing higher-order competencies in leadership, empathy, and social analysis. For faculty and administrators, it utilizes their pedagogical expertise as they mentor students, while simultaneously utilizing their skills aligned with their professional experience and expertise.

Crucially, the program utilizes the invaluable indigenous knowledge of our community partners as local experts, and in the process, develops their own competencies in co-program management and youth mentorship.

Criterion 4. The students' experiences in community engagement and service activities are processed and integrated into the different academic programs.

Rating: 4

Community engagement is fully integrated into the academic curriculum through formation-focused subjects.

Guided by the Church’s Pastoral Cycle, the Arrupe Office developed the Pakighinabi – Pamalandong – Adbokasiya framework, which processes students’ community engagement and service activities from experience to reflection and eventually to concrete action. This tool is integrated into the academic programs.

The Catholic Social Teachings (CST) subject provides the theological and ethical foundation by using the Pastoral Cycle (experience–reflection–action) to allow students to analyze social issues more deeply, understand the realities faced by partner communities, and explore possible responses. Patterned after this is the Pakighinabi–Pamalandong–Adbokasiya that guides students from encounter to reflection to concrete action for the common good. The Leadership Development Program 3 and 4 strengthens this by giving students opportunities to design and implement advocacy initiatives, such as creating income-generating activities that support both their learning and the needs of the community. After each engagement, students immediately process their experiences through reflection activities and evaluations, ensuring that learning is both personal and meaningful.

This integration ensures that community engagement is not treated as an extracurricular activity but as a core pedagogical tool, allowing students to connect classroom learning with lived realities, fostering both intellectual formation and a strong sense of social responsibility, while contributing to the empowerment of partner communities.

Criterion 5. An appropriate structure with adequate resources is in place to support community engagement and service activities.

Rating: 4

The school, through its Formation Office, has established the Arrupe Office for Social Formation (AOSF) as the lead unit for community engagement and service. Guided by its Arrupe Office for Social Formation - Manual of Operations, the school ensures that community engagement is systematically implemented, sustained, and integrated into student formation.

To support these initiatives, the school allocates a dedicated portion of its annual budget for the office’s operations and programs. The Arrupe Office is manned by qualified personnel with expertise in social involvement and community development and works in close coordination with the Theology Cluster and Leadership Development Program faculty to enrich its initiatives.

In addition, a community engagement fee is collected from students to guarantee the sustainability, effectiveness, and continuity of partnerships and engagements with communities.

Criterion 6. Community engagement and service activities are systematically monitored and evaluated against established criteria.

Rating: 4

The Arrupe Office for Social Formation (AOSF) implements a structured monitoring and evaluation system for the Senior High School’s community engagement activities. Monitoring is done through attendance, documentation, and facilitator supervision during activities, while evaluation is carried out after each engagement through student reflections, evaluation forms, teacher and moderator reports, and community partner feedback. Results are consolidated into semestral and annual reports.

All activities are assessed against established criteria as discussed thoroughly in the Arrupe Office for Social Formation - Manual of Operations: (1) Student Learning and Formation, (2) Effectiveness of Implementation, (3) Integration of Reflection and Faith-Justice Values, and (4) Sustainability and Partnership-Building. This process ensures that programs are accountable, responsive to community needs, and formative for students.
