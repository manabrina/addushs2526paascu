Criterion 1. A strategic planning process involving the different members of the school community is undertaken periodically.

Rating: 4.5

The University ensures broad stakeholder involvement in the periodic crafting of its strategic plans. Under the One Ateneo, One Plan (1A1P) Strategic Plan for 2018-2023.

The University ensures broad stakeholder involvement in the framework, administrators, personnel, students, and other internal stakeholders actively participated through discussions, volunteer initiatives, and engagements with marginalized groups in Mindanao. These efforts shaped programs on faith, social justice, and culture. Partnerships with local organizations, government agencies, civil society groups, and religious organizations further strengthened initiatives in education, environment, governance, and social inclusion. Alumni and external partners also contributed to leadership development, lifelong learning, and the advancement of the University’s mission.

For Fortiores 2030, administrators, faculty, staff, students, and trustees—together with external experts and resource persons—participated in planning conferences and community convocation. This inclusive approach contextualized strategic directions within regional, national, and global realities. Grounded in Ignatian spirituality, the process emphasized communal discernment and shared governance to ensure the plan reflected the collective aspirations of the University community.

Overall, the University’s strategic planning is dynamic, participatory, and mission-driven, requiring only minor refinements to achieve fuller integration of all stakeholder voices.

Criterion 2. Relevant internal and external conditions are identified and considered in the formulation of strategic goals and objectives.

Rating: 4.5

The School contributes to the University’s strategic goals by evaluating both internal and external conditions in line with its vision and mission. Internal scan (student and faculty profiles, resources) informs program development, administrative coordination, faculty and infrastructure growth, and advancements in science and technology. External assessments include political and economic conditions, the educational landscape, and the situation of marginalized communities in Mindanao, particularly the Bangsamoro, and Lumad. These guide initiatives on social justice, sustainability, technopreneurship, international partnerships, and academe-industry integration, supported by Pakighinabi conversations.

Within the Fortiores 2030, the University continues to align strategic goals with its identity as a Jesuit, Catholic, and Filipino institution. Internally, this involves recognizing its strengths, culture, programs, accomplishments, and challenges identified in the One Ateneo, One Plan. Externally, it incorporates the socio-political landscape of Mindanao, regional development efforts, national education trends, and global concerns on environment and social justice.

Criterion 8. A system for the periodic evaluation of plans, programs, and activities is in place, and adjustments to the strategic goals and the execution of these are made as needed.

Rating: 4.5

Ateneo de Davao University Senior High School (SHS) maintains a systematic and participatory framework for the periodic evaluation of plans, programs, and activities, ensuring continuous alignment with strategic goals. The process is anchored in a common evaluation cycle that includes year-end evaluations, complemented by year-end consolidated submissions.

Evaluation begins at the student level through learning portfolios, performance logs, and formative feedback, which are consolidated at the cluster level to assess academic achievement, program effectiveness, and extracurricular engagement. At the unit level, Heads of Office evaluate their respective functions and report results to the Principal, who forwards them to the administration. These evaluations are then integrated across the AFIRE dimensions—Administration, Formation, Instruction, Research, and Engagement—and submitted to the Vice President for Quality Assurance for institutional oversight.

Objective analysis is supported by structured tools such as surveys, rubrics, and key performance indicators (KPIs), which measure outcomes against strategic targets. Evaluation results are communicated transparently through Parent Meetings, Faculty Assemblies, and Student Government forums, creating feedback loops that promote accountability, shared ownership, and collaborative decision-making.

Aligned with the One Ateneo, One Plan (1A1P) Strategic Framework for 2018-2023 and the Fortiores 2030, this evaluation system embeds flexibility to respond to emerging challenges and opportunities. Adjustments to strategic goals and operational priorities are made as needed, reinforcing SHS’s culture of continuous improvement, evidence-based governance, and responsiveness to the evolving educational landscape.
