Criterion 1. The Board of Principals or Board of Trustees, as well as the administrators, are qualified for their respective roles.

Rating: 5

The governance and administrative framework of Ateneo de Davao University Senior High School is anchored on a qualified Board of Trustees and Basic Education Council, whose members possess the academic credentials, professional experience, and leadership competence required of their roles. The Board of Trustees provides strategic direction grounded in sound educational principles and aligned with the University’s mission and vision.

Senior High School Administrators are vested with clear mandates and supported by specialized administrators and staff to ensure effective school management.

This governance structure enables responsive and visionary policies that directly support the delivery of quality senior high school education. Leadership practices foster a culture of excellence, integrity, and accountability, ensuring that AdDU SHS remains responsive to the needs of its students and the wider community.

Criterion 3. The School Principal must hold a master's degree and should have at least five (5) years of relevant teaching or administrative experience.

Rating: 5

The School Principal of Ateneo de Davao University Senior High School, Dr. Joan Grace A. Palconit, fully meets and exceeds the required qualifications for her position. She holds a Master of Arts in Education, major in Filipino (Holy Cross of Davao College, 2007), and a Doctor of Philosophy in Applied Linguistics (University of the Immaculate Conception, 2014).

Dr. Palconit has served the Ateneo de Davao University system for over 21 years, with extensive academic and administrative experience. Her leadership journey includes roles as Filipino Coordinator, Academic Personnel for Teaching in Humanities and Liberal Arts, and Cluster Leader for Grade 12 Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) Strand, culminating in her appointment as Senior High School Principal in June 2025.

Her advanced educational attainment, coupled with her depth of institutional experience, demonstrates substantial competence and readiness for leadership.

Criterion 4. The Administration Promotes Good Governance, Integrity, and Accountability.

Rating: 4.5

The administration of Ateneo de Davao University Senior High School upholds good governance through clear policies, well-defined organizational structures, and participative decision-making processes. The Faculty Club Manual and Administrative Manual provide comprehensive guidelines on roles, responsibilities, and conduct, fostering professionalism among administrators, faculty, and staff. Transparent communication is further ensured through formal channels, meetings, and circulars that guide collective action and strengthen institutional leadership.

Integrity is safeguarded by the Ethics Board and specialized committees that address conflicts and uphold ethical standards with impartiality, due process, and confidentiality. The school also implements proactive measures such as the Safe Spaces Act, Gender and Development seminars, and guidelines on responsible use of artificial intelligence, reflecting its commitment to a respectful, inclusive, and forward-looking academic environment. Compliance with Department of Education directives and national mandates aligns governance practices with broader standards of accountability and quality.

Accountability is institutionalized through systematic documentation, financial stewardship, regular reporting, and adherence to policies on personnel and student welfare. Periodic evaluations and feedback mechanisms ensure responsibility for outcomes, strengthen trust, and sustain stakeholder confidence.

Criterion 5. Leadership is consultative and participative.

Rating: 5

The school demonstrates participatory governance by institutionalizing consultative committees and structured assemblies across administration, formation, and instruction. These mechanisms provide formal venues for dialogue, collaboration, and shared accountability, ensuring transparency and alignment with the school’s mission.

Administrative committees, led by the Assistant Principal for Administration, convene administrators, faculty, staff, parents, and student leaders in regular assemblies. Parent participation is further reinforced through finance committees, convergence meetings, and assemblies, highlighting their role as active partners in school life. Formative and instructional committees sustain the consultative character of the institution. The Assistant Principal for Formation collaborates with faculty, formation heads, and student leaders to strengthen holistic development initiatives, while the Assistant Principal for Academics works with Cluster Leaders and teachers to refine curriculum, pedagogy, and assessment.

Through the establishment of consultative and participative committees, the school demonstrates an integrated and inclusive system of governance. Active engagement of administrators, faculty, parents, and students ensures responsiveness to community needs, reinforces shared accountability, and strengthens the institution’s commitment to holistic formation and academic excellence.

Criterion 6. Leadership is proactive in anticipating developments and responding to changes that may affect the school's programs and operations.

Rating: 5

The school demonstrates proactive adjustments by anticipating developments and responding effectively to changes that affect its academic, administrative, and formative programs. This readiness enabled the smooth adoption of the K–12 Curriculum under the One Ateneo, One Plan framework, which advanced faith formation, social justice, environmental sustainability, and academic excellence.

The school shows adaptability by sustaining learning continuity and stakeholder engagement even during periods of disruption. Flexible approaches were implemented to maintain academic and co-curricular activities, while alignment with government directives ensured smooth transitions across learning modalities. Further, the school’s active participation in the DepEd Strengthened SHS Curriculum Pilot Testing highlights its commitment to meeting evolving educational standards and trends.

Likewise, the Fortiores 2030 reinforces the school’s vigilance in monitoring local, national, and global developments. Grounded in Ignatian values, it promotes creativity, collaboration, and values-based decision-making as foundations for resilience and innovation. This forward-looking stance affirms leadership’s commitment to ensuring that programs and operations remain mission-driven, relevant, and sustainable.

Criterion 8. Provisions for Leadership Succession are in place.

Rating: 3.5

The Ateneo Succession Planning and Management Committee (ASPMC) has been established to coordinate and oversee the University’s succession planning system, ensuring leadership continuity and institutional sustainability. It analyzes current and projected human resource needs and identifies key positions critical to maintaining effective operations. The committee defines competency requirements for these roles and assesses leadership potential through evaluations and recommendations. It develops tailored leadership development plans and recommends qualified successors to appointing authorities. The ASPMC also facilitates leadership transitions, communicates plans to affected teams, and monitors successors’ post-transition adjustment. It collaborates with the Office of Strategic Management and Quality Assurance to evaluate and improve the succession planning system regularly.
