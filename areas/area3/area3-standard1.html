Criterion 1. The school has adequate and qualified teaching personnel

Rating: 4.5

Ateneo de Davao University – Senior High School is adequately staffed with highly qualified teaching personnel who are capable of delivering quality instruction. For School Year 2025 -2026, the teacher-to-student ratio is 1:28.

The Teacher’s Loading Policy and Inventory demonstrate that teaching assignments are strategically aligned with each teacher’s academic qualifications, area of specialization, and professional experience.

The ADDU–SHS Academic Personnel Profile contains comprehensive records of each teacher’s academic background, professional credentials, job description, number of teaching hours per week, and licensing status. Of the one hundred thirty-eight (138) teachers, one hundred thirty-two (132) or 95.6% are licensed. Six (6) teachers are currently taking the required education units in preparation for the Licensure Examination for Teachers (LET).

Supporting Evidences:

  • 3.1.1.1 ADDU-SHS Academic Personnel Profile
  • 3.1.1.2 Teacher’s Loading Inventory

Criterion 2. The school has adequate and qualified non-teaching personnel

Rating: 4.5

The school maintains an adequate and competent complement of non-teaching personnel who provide vital administrative, clerical, technical, and maintenance support to the institution.

The Non-Teaching Personnel Directory, which includes each staff member’s respective office or functional assignment, confirms that the staffing level is sufficient to meet the school’s daily operational needs. Personnel are deployed in alignment with their skills, training, and competencies, ensuring efficiency and effectiveness in delivering support services to both learners and employees.

The 201 Files of Non-Teaching Personnel contain complete documentation of each staff member’s educational background, professional qualifications, employment history, and other pertinent records, confirming their suitability for their assigned roles.

Supporting Evidences:

  • Non-Teaching Personnel Directory

Criterion 3. Recruitment, selection, hiring, and deployment policies and practices are in place. These are formulated, communicated to all concerned, and consistently implemented.

Rating: 4.5

Through the Human Resource Management and Development Office (HRMDO), the school has well-defined policies and procedures for recruitment, selection, hiring, and deployment, as documented in the Policies and Procedures on Recruitment, Selection, Hiring, and Deployment in the HRMDO Manual of Operations. These policies ensure that only qualified and competent personnel are hired, with selection based on the actual staffing needs of the school. Importantly, the policies and procedures are expressed in the HRMDO Manual of Operations, which is downloadable from klippert.addu.edu.ph and can be accessed by all employees.

Implementation of these policies is monitored through reports from the HRMDO and from the respective Strand Chairpersons. A key tool in this process is the Teacher’s Loading Inventory, which details the number of teaching loads per faculty member, serving as the basis for determining whether additional teaching staff are required for the upcoming academic term.

Personnel Request Form, specifying the required number of teachers and non-teaching staff, is completed for human resource needs.

These procedures, supported by documented policies, careful workload analysis, and formal requests, demonstrate a consistent, needs-based, and competency-driven approach to staffing.

Supporting Evidences:

  • Teacher’s Loading Inventory
  • Personnel Request

Criterion 4. Development and training programs for teaching and non-teaching personnel are needs-based and are made available to both full-time and part-time personnel.

Rating: 4.5

The school, through the Staff, Teachers, and Administrators Development Coordinator (STADECO), ensures that its faculty development and training programs are responsive to the specific needs of the teaching staff. Training initiatives are needs-based, with topics identified through multiple channels, including participants’ feedback from previous In-Service Training (INSET) evaluations, operational requirements, needs-based survey, and emerging institutional priorities.

As reflected in the Development and Training Program Report, these programs aim to enhance the professional competencies and overall effectiveness of personnel. The INSET Program Post-Evaluation provides evidence of the relevance and impact of the sessions, while the INSET Attendance Sheets confirm active participation from both full-time and part-time personnel. This inclusive approach ensures equal access to professional growth opportunities for all staff members.

For non-teaching personnel, the Human Resource Management and Development Office (HRMDO) delivers training through the ADDU-RISE platform. NTPs can select from a wide range of programs, including basic fundamentals, skills-based, career, personal development, external, formation, and engagement trainings.

Together, these documented systems and evidence demonstrate that both teaching and non-teaching personnel are provided inclusive, needs-based opportunities for professional and personal growth.

Supporting Evidences:

  • Development and Training Program Report
  • In- Service Training (INSET) Program Post-evaluation
  • INSET attendance sheets
  • Needs Assessment
  • ADDU-RISE

Criterion 5. Career pathing programs and provisions for advancement are in place.

Rating: 4.5

The school has a 5-Year Comprehensive Faculty Development Plan that serves as the framework for career pathing and advancement. This plan provides systematic opportunities for growth, skills enhancement, and leadership development for teaching faculty, who are clustered for targeted training, as well as for formation personnel.

For non-teaching personnel, career pathing is supported through ADDU-RISE, which offers a wide range of programs for skills development, career progression, and personal growth. This ensures that both academic and non-academic staff are given clear and inclusive pathways for advancement.

In line with its commitment to leadership development and continuity, the university is also taking steps toward the formation of a Succession Planning and Management Committee. This initiative reflects its intention to sustain long-term career growth and leadership opportunities across all personnel groups.

Supporting Evidences:

  • ADDU-SHS 5-year Comprehensive Faculty Development Plan
  • ADDU-RISE
  • Memo: Formation of the Ateneo Succession Planning and Management Committee

Criterion 6. The school implements a performance management system that includes coaching and mentoring, regular performance evaluation, as well as recognition for meritorious performance.

Rating: 4.5

The school implements a well-structured performance management system designed to enhance the professional growth and effectiveness of its personnel. The system integrates coaching and mentoring sessions, conducted by Chairpersons and Cluster Leaders (CLs), to provide targeted support and guidance. Regular performance evaluations are carried out, with results documented in Post-Conference Evaluation Forms, ensuring that feedback is both systematic and actionable.

The Rank-and-Promotion Policy outlines clear criteria and procedures for recognizing and rewarding meritorious performance. This policy is operationalized through periodic re-ranking of teachers, as evidenced by the List of Re-Ranked Teachers, which reflects the school’s commitment to acknowledging outstanding contributions and sustained excellence.

Service and research awards recognize loyalty, excellence, and innovation. Service awards promote commitment and stability, while research awards encourage professional growth and scholarly contribution. As One Basic Education, the school is also developing an institutionalized awards system to further merit and recognize outstanding employees across all basic education units.

Supporting Evidences:

  • Post-conference Evaluation Forms from Chairpersons and CLs
  • Rank-and-Promo Policy
  • List of Re-ranked Teachers

Criterion 7. Salaries, benefits, and other incentives are set at levels that ensure the school’s ability to attract and retain qualified personnel.

Rating: 4.5

The school offers competitive salaries, comprehensive benefits, and additional incentives that enable the institution to attract and retain highly qualified personnel. The Salary Scale—regularly reviewed and updated over the past eight years—demonstrates the school’s commitment to fair, equitable, and market-responsive compensation.

The Employees’ Other Benefits (EOB), which include tuition fee discounts for employees pursuing graduate studies and for their dependents, health insurance, leave entitlements, uniform allowance, optical allowance, and other allowances, further enhance employee welfare.

These measures contribute to high staff morale and long-term retention, as reflected in the attrition rate, which show a significant number of personnel with extended tenure.

Supporting Evidences:

  • Salary Scale for the last 8 years
  • EOB
  • Length of Service of Employees
  • Memo on increases

Criterion 8. Policies and practices related to resignation, termination of employment, and retirement are clearly formulated, communicated to all concerned, and consistently implemented.

Rating: 4

The school has established clear and comprehensive policies governing resignation, termination of employment, and retirement. These policies are consistently implemented and ensure that processes are transparent, fair, and aligned with institutional standards. To promote accessibility and awareness, the policies are expressed in the HRMDO Manual of Operations, which is downloadable via klippert.addu.edu.ph and available to all employees.

In support of retired personnel, the Ateneo de Davao Organization of Retirees and Former Employees, Inc. (ADORE) provides a platform for retirees and former employees to connect, remain engaged, and maintain their ties with the Ateneo de Davao community. In addition, the SHS Faculty Club has also implemented programs to honor its retirees, as demonstrated during the retirement of its former principal, reflecting the school’s recognition of dedicated service.

Supporting Evidences:

  • Policy related to resignation, termination of employment, and retirement
  • ADORE
  • Documntation: Retirement Program

Criterion 9. The working environment conforms to applicable standards and regulations that promote the safety, security, and well-being of personnel.

Rating: 4

The school ensures that the working environment complies with national and institutional standards that safeguard the safety, security, and well-being of its personnel. As reflected in the List of Programs and Policies that Promote the Safety, Security, and Well-being, Safeguarding, Policy on Protection of Vulnerable Adults, OSH, CODI, Drug-Free Workplace Policy and Program are in place to support workplace safety and wellness. The Proof of Implementation provides confirmation that these programs are carried out, while the Photo Documentation and Utilization Reports demonstrate active participation of both teaching and non-teaching personnel. These pieces of evidence collectively affirm the institution’s commitment to maintaining a safe, secure, and supportive environment for all employees.

Supporting Evidences:

  • List of programs that promote the safety, security, and well-being of faculty and staff
  • Proof of implementation
  • List of photo documentation and utilization report on program participation

Criterion 10. Policies and programs related to the recruitment, selection, hiring, deployment, development, and retirement of personnel are periodically evaluated and improved.

Rating: 4

The school evaluates and updates its policies and programs related to the recruitment, selection, hiring, deployment, development, and retirement of personnel. These evaluations ensure that practices remain relevant, fair, and aligned with the school's goals and current labor standards. Continuous improvements are made based on feedback, performance data, and changing workforce needs, promoting a responsive and sustainable HR system.

Supporting Evidences:

  • Evaluation report on policies related to recruitment, selection, hiring, deployment, development, and retirement of personnel
  • Policies related to recruitment, selection, hiring, deployment, development and retirement of personnel
