Criterion 1. Physical facilities are adequate and conducive to teaching and learning, research work, and community service activities.

Rating: 4.8

The Ateneo de Davao Senior University - Senior High School physical facilities are adequate and conducive to teaching and learning, research work, and community service activities.

Inventory of Physical Facilities includes a complete array of academic, administrative, and auxiliary spaces located across strategically designed buildings. The school has an adequate number of classrooms; it maintains eight (8) unutilized classrooms that are ready for occupancy when needed. As the primary centers of learning, each is equipped with an LCD projector, LED TV, tables and chairs, whiteboards, and full air-conditioning.

Significant among the facilities are the library with its audio-visual room and collaboration rooms, the science laboratories, one each for Physics, Biology, Chemistry, Computer, and Technical Drawing Room, which have provided learners with hands-on learning experiences. Adequate laboratory space, safety features, and modern apparatus ensure effective instruction, encourage experimentation, and promote collaborative learning. The school has set aside provisions for additional science laboratories in the near future.

The large gymnasium and the Olympic-size swimming pool support and enhance the physical education and sports programs of the school. The gymnasium is also available as a venue for symposia on social issues and preparations for the community service activities, such as gathering and packing of relief goods.

The Christ the King Chapel, prominently located at the center of the campus, provides a spiritual anchor to the school’s physical layout and reflects the institution’s commitment to holistic development. Its program includes a monthly Mass that is open to the public, especially to the residents of the Bangkal community.

The presence of these major facilities demonstrates the school’s commitment to providing a conducive and well-rounded learning environment.

Criterion 2. There is an office or personnel responsible for overseeing the maintenance, safety, and development of physical facilities.

Rating: 5.0

The school has a clearly designated office, the Physical Plant Office (PPO), that is primarily responsible for ensuring the maintenance, safety, and continuous development of the school’s physical facilities. This office is supervised by a qualified engineer, who brings technical expertise to the role, and is supported by an associate staff member. The PPO supervisor plays a central role in coordinating with external service providers, janitorial and security agencies, to ensure the cleanliness, safety, and functionality of the campus environment.

The Assistant Principal for Administration also plays a vital role in the management and oversight of school facilities. The Assistant Principal for Administration works closely with the PPO Supervisor to monitor conditions, propose improvements, and resolve facility-related concerns efficiently. This collaborative setup ensures that both the operational (day-to-day maintenance and safety) and strategic (planning and development) aspects of facility management are addressed.

Criterion 3. Classrooms, lecture halls, and special-purpose rooms are adequate, kept clean, free from distractions, and conducive to teaching and learning.

Rating: 5.0

The school maintains 91 classrooms, 4 lecture halls, 2 conference rooms, an auditorium, and a Quality Assurance Room. They are conducive to learning. These spaces are regularly cleaned and maintained, ensuring that they remain free from distractions and support a focused academic environment. They are also fully air-conditioned.

The Inventory of Classrooms, Lecture Halls, and Special-Purpose Rooms confirms that the facilities are sufficient to accommodate the current student population, while the Deployment Plan of Maintenance Workers demonstrates systematic upkeep and cleaning schedules. Additionally, halls are properly maintained and provide visual evidence of the institution’s commitment to a tidy, organized, and safe learning environment.

These efforts ensure that all instructional spaces are not only functionally adequate but also meet standards of cleanliness and orderliness, fostering optimal conditions for teaching and learning.

Criterion 4. The library facilities and audio-visual rooms are adequate, accessible, safe, and up-to-date.

Rating: 3.9

The school ensures that its library facilities and audio-visual rooms are adequately equipped, accessible, safe, and maintained to support teaching, learning, and independent study. Their adequacy and condition are documented in the Floor Plan and Dimensions of Library Facilities and Audio-Visual Rooms.

In compliance with the Standards for Philippine Libraries, the school library exceeds the requirement of accommodating 10% of the total student population. The library has an updated collection of physical books and resources. Moreover, a virtual library is also available to meet online needs. The library also has computers, laptops, and collaboration rooms.

The Inventory of Materials and Equipment of the Library Facilities and Audio-Visual Rooms verifies the availability of learning resources and technological equipment, while the Evacuation Plan outlines safety measures for these spaces. The library facilities and audio-visual rooms are sufficient in capacity, properly equipped, safe, and well-maintained.

Criterion 6. The space in the school canteen is adequate for the school population.

Rating: 4.0

The school ensures that its canteen facilities are adequate to comfortably accommodate the entire student population while maintaining a safe and orderly dining environment. Two canteens, strategically located in the Student Town Center (STC) and Building E, serve students across all grade levels.

The Floor Plan and Dimension of School Canteen confirm that the total floor area is adequate for the expected number of diners at any given time, while the Inventory of Tables and Chairs in the School Canteen verifies the availability of sufficient seating and dining furniture to meet student needs.

To further ensure comfort and prevent overcrowding, the class schedule is strategically arranged so that Grade 11 and Grade 12 learners have separate lunch breaks. This staggered schedule allows for a smooth flow of students, minimizes waiting time, and ensures that every learner is properly accommodated.

Criterion 12. There are sufficient provisions in the school’s physical facilities for ensuring the safety, security, health, and well-being of learners and personnel.

Rating: 4.0

The school provides sufficient physical facilities and measures to ensure the safety, security, health, and well-being of learners and personnel.

The well-designed railings are strategically placed in all buildings to address the risk for falling. They are checked regularly for stability. Warning signs are properly displayed near restricted or hazardous areas.

Drop-off and pick-up points are clearly identified. Speed signs within the campus are adequate and strategically positioned. Pedestrian lanes are clearly visible. Security personnel are designated to control traffic flow and the crossing of students and employees.

CCTV cameras are installed in strategic non-intrusive places. Adequate security personnel monitor the entry and exit points of the school where metal detectors are placed.

Evacuation and emergency exits are strategically displayed for easier reference. Fire extinguishers are adequate and accessible. Fire alarms and smoke detectors are installed and regularly tested.

Sanitation and water facilities are maintained. Gender-segregated toilets are adequate and kept clean. Provisions for toilet paper and soap are adequate. Hand sanitizers or alcohol are provided in strategic areas of the school. Water fountains are filtered and regularly tested.

The clinic is equipped with a complete facility that includes beds, an isolation room, basic medication and equipment, and a competent team of nurses and doctors.

Criterion 13. The school is compliant with regulatory and statutory government requirements as far as physical facilities are concerned.

Rating: 3.9

The school is compliant with regulatory and statutory government requirements, as evidenced by the occupancy permits granted to the school buildings. The Fire Safety Inspection Certificate is likewise secured from the Bureau of Fire.

In addition, the school has passed the water potability test, confirming that its water supply is safe and fit for consumption. The campus elevator also undergoes regular preventive maintenance through Hyundai, ensuring safety, reliability, and compliance with operational requirements.

These measures reflect the school’s consistent adherence to government standards and its commitment to maintaining safe, functional, and compliant physical facilities.

Criterion 14. Facilities and provisions that cater to persons with special needs are available.

Rating: 3.9

The school provides facilities and provisions that accommodate persons with special needs, ensuring accessibility and inclusivity in all areas.

Ramps are available for those who use wheelchairs. Likewise, elevators are provided to ensure that all individuals, especially persons with special needs, the elderly, pregnant women, and those with mobility challenges, can move freely and safely between the five (5) floors of a building.

Provisions for learners with special needs are made through accommodations of classroom assignments on the ground floor.

Protocols for learners with mental health concerns are in place to respond to such needs.
